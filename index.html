Profile

About me

I study mathematical and statistical models designed to describe irregularity, persistence and risk in financial time series.

My academic background combines theoretical physics, probability theory and mathematical finance. My research focuses on the use of fractional and multifractional stochastic processes in financial modelling.

A central theme of my work is the relation between local regularity, dependence structures and volatility. I develop statistical methodologies for estimating the Hurst exponent and for identifying rough, persistent and antipersistent market regimes.

I am also interested in high-frequency financial data, realized volatility, market efficiency and the empirical comparison of classical and fractional volatility models.

Research

Research interests

My current research activity is organised around the following interconnected areas.

01

Fractional processes

Fractional Brownian motion, fractional Gaussian noise, multifractional processes, self-similarity and long-range dependence.

02

Rough volatility

Statistical identification of rough market regimes and fractional approaches to realized and implied volatility.

03

Financial econometrics

High-frequency data, volatility forecasting, goodness-of-fit testing and estimation of dependence and regularity parameters.

04

Hölder regularity

Local regularity as a tool for analysing financial risk, market states and time-varying volatility structures.

05

Market efficiency

Statistical assessment of efficient and inefficient market regimes through memory, scaling and local regularity.

06

Statistical methods

Kolmogorov–Smirnov procedures, Monte Carlo analysis, fractional differentiation and robust estimation for dependent processes.
